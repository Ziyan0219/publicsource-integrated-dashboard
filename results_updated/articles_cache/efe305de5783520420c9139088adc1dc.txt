Pittsburgh’s mayoral primary options: Corey O’Connor by Charlie Wolfson, Pittsburgh's Public Source March 4, 2025 <h1>Pittsburgh’s mayoral primary options: Corey O’Connor</h1>
<p class="byline">by Charlie Wolfson, Pittsburgh's Public Source <br />March 4, 2025</p>
					<br />
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/9.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1316262" style="width:700px" /></figure>
<div class="wp-block-group is-style-default has-dark-gray-color has-text-color has-link-color">
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Age: 40</li>
<li>Home neighborhood: Point Breeze</li>
<li>Political affiliation: Democrat</li>
<li>Elected experience: Pittsburgh city councilor from 2012-2022, Allegheny County controller since 2023</li>
</ul></div>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/mayoral-basebal.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1316247" /></figure>
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/12.png" alt="Text on image that says: PLATFORM HIGHLIGHTS." class="wp-image-1316265" /></figure>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Reforming permit and zoning systems to accelerate new housing construction</li>
<li>Expanding youth programming and investing in community centers</li>
<li>Adding staffing and funding to public safety bureaus</li>
</ul>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/mayoral-basebal.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1316247" /></figure>
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/13.png" alt="Text on image that says: SIGNATURE ACHIEVEMENTS." class="wp-image-1316266" /></figure>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>As a City Council member, championed a <a href="https://archive.triblive.com/news/pittsburgh-city-council-approves-ordinance-requiring-paid-sick-leave/#axzz3hm8CiQAH">paid sick leave bill</a> and <a href="https://www.publicsource.org/pittsburgh-reveals-gun-control-bills-promised-after-tree-of-life-shooting/">gun control measures</a></li>
<li>As controller, led audits of the county’s indigent defense system, Clean Air Fund and homeless services</li>
</ul>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/mayoral-basebal.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1316247" /></figure>
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full is-resized"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/14.png" alt="Text on image that says: QUOTE." class="wp-image-1316267" style="width:900px" /></figure>
<p>“There is no vision coming off of Grant Street anymore,” O’Connor said at his December campaign announcement. “Instead, when I look around, I see a mayor and an administration that is managing decline instead of working to grow Pittsburgh.”</p>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/mayoral-basebal.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1316247" /></figure>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Incumbent mayors running for re-election in Pittsburgh have lost just once in the past century (though that one time was four years ago).</li>
<li>He lacks support from top progressive leaders and organizations.</li>
</ul>
<figure class="wp-block-image size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/mayoral-basebal.png" alt="" class="wp-image-1316247" /></figure>
<figure class="wp-block-image aligncenter size-full"><img src="https://www.publicsource.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/16.png" alt="Text on image that says: PROMINENT ENDORSEMENTS." class="wp-image-1316269" /></figure>
<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Allegheny County Democratic Committee, Young Democrats of Allegheny County, 14th Ward Independent Democratic Club</li>
<li>Pennsylvania Laborers’ Council, unions representing ironworkers, steamfitters, plumbers, boilermakers, Pittsburgh paramedics and EMTs and others</li>
<li>City Councilors Erika Strassburger, Bobby Wilson, Bob Charland, Theresa Kail-Smith and Anthony Coghill, State Sen. Wayne Fontana, former City Councilors Ricky Burgess and Bruce Kraus</li>
</ul>

<ul class="wp-block-list">
<li>Has coached golf at his alma mater of Central Catholic High School for 17 years, winning three state titles.</li>
</ul>
