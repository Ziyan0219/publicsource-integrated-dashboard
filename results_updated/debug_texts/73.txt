The General Election for Pittsburgh mayor, County Council and more is Nov. 4. by Charlie Wolfson March 12, 2025 August 18, 2025 Share this: Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window) Facebook Click to share on X (Opens in new window) X Pittsburgh voters will elect a mayor this year, deciding between Democratic nominee Corey O’Connor and Republican nominee Tony Moreno. More than a third of Allegheny County Council seats are on the ballot this year, along with five of nine Pittsburgh Public Schools board seats and four of nine Pittsburgh City Council seats. Voters registered as Democrats and Republicans went to the polls May 20 for primary elections to choose nominees for the Nov. 4 General Election. Independent and minor party candidates had until Aug. 1 to file to be on the November ballot. Allegheny County voters will also choose eight new judges for the Court of Common Pleas
