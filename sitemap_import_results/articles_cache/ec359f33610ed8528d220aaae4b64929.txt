Hollowed out How Pittsburgh-based EQT’s expansion in West Virginia set four families reeling, while state regulators trusted the company to answer their complaints EQT expanded its natural gas fracking operations in Knob Fork, a hamlet in West Virginia, in early 2021 — two years ahead of a $5.2 billion acquisition that will deepen the firm’s Appalachian operations. By August 2021, Knob Fork families were writing to West Virginia environmental regulators. Within months, they were pleading for help as health complaints mounted. By mid-2022, families were fleeing the hollow, abandoning longtime homes.
